Hello! I'm Dale Schofield, an IT major at NJIT!

I'm currently a junior and my IT specialization is Game Development.

Thanks for stopping by and taking the time to read this! Through both my love of gaming and computers, as well as my challenging yet enriching courses at NJIT, I aspire to be a game developer in the future. Specifically, I would like to become a gameplay programmer or work with the game engine, being a part of the foundation and working with others to build a great game.
If you take a look through my projects, you'll see two game engine projects I've worked on, one in 3D and one in 2D as well as the start of my NJIT game dev journey, my Quake 4 mod, as well as the latest project from my educational software design course, Minion Farming Simulator 2020. I'm very proud of all of these projects. Each one taught me something new and built upon the last.
Through these projects, I've gained valuable experience with Visual Studio, Unity, GitHub, Blender, Linear Algebra, C, C++, and C#. I've also had courses in Game Design and Architecture, teaching me more about game design itself, as well as game economies, narratives, and how choices impact a player's decisions.

I'm also currently the vice-president of NJIT Esports, the campus' main gaming club for both casual and competitive players. I love interacting with our community, so you can often find me helping out at our events!
I was there at the founding of NJIT Esports my first semester at NJIT, and have been along for the ride since. I've gotten to experience the ups and downs of working with my peers, managing their tasks, talking to administration, planning events, and everything else that comes with such a position. It's given me experience in not only management, but also socializing and brainstorming, two very important skills in any workplace.
